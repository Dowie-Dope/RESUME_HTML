    Commuters

    Given the current state of traffic and public transportation in the Philippines, many people are seeking more efficient and comfortable ways to navigate the city. One popular solution is using motorcycles. Life on two wheels has become quite familiar to many of us. We might have our own motorcycle that we take out for weekend rides, or we may know someone who rides either for leisure or daily errands.

    Money savings

    The primary reason to commute by motorcycle is cost savings compared to cars or ride-hailing services. Motorcycles can be bought for under P100,000, while cars cost several hundred thousand pesos. Parking in Metro Manila is around P40 per day for motorcycles versus P200 for cars. Additionally, motorcycles average 30-45 kilometers per liter, compared to cars' 7-12 kilometers per liter. Commuting daily from Quezon City to Makati costs P670 per month for motorcycle fuel, versus P2500 for a car, a nearly 4x saving.

    3

    Personal time

    Another reason to commute by motorcycle is the opportunity for personal time. Riding demands full concentration and effort, engaging all four limbs and detaching you from gadgets and distractions. This focus on the road allows riders to enjoy solitude and mental privacy. If you seek personal time and mental space, commuting by motorcycle can be a rewarding choice.
